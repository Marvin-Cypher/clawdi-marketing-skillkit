Blog post:


Artificial intelligence tools have evolved quickly over the past few years. Many people first encountered AI through chatbots that could answer questions, write emails, or generate content.
But a new category of AI systems is now emerging: AI agents.
While chatbots focus on conversation, AI agents are designed to take actions. And understanding the difference between the two helps explain where AI tools are heading next.
What Is a Chatbot?
A chatbot is an AI system designed primarily for conversation. You ask questions or give prompts, and the system generates responses. 
Chatbots are commonly used for tasks like answering customer support questions, generating written content, explaining information, and brainstorming ideas. These systems are useful, but they usually operate inside a conversation interface. They do not typically interact directly with other software tools.
What Is an AI Agent?
An AI agent goes beyond conversation. Instead of simply generating responses, an agent can observe, reason, and take actions. For example, an AI agent might read incoming emails, extract important information, schedule meetings, generate reports, and update software tools.
Rather than stopping at a response, the agent continues the workflow by performing tasks.
Why AI Agents Are Becoming More Popular
As AI models become more capable, developers are increasingly connecting them to external systems. This allows AI assistants to interact with email platforms, calendars, project management tools, databases, and web services.
The result is AI systems that can automate parts of real workflows. Instead of simply suggesting what to do next, the AI can actually do it.
Where Chatbots Still Make Sense
Chatbots are still useful in many situations. They work well for customer support conversations, knowledge bases, writing assistance, and learning and research. And in these cases, the goal is simply to provide information or generate content.
No additional actions are required.
Where AI Agents Excel
AI agents are particularly powerful when tasks involve multiple steps, such as managing communication, coordinating schedules, summarizing documents, and automating repetitive workflows. These are areas where traditional chatbots often fall short because they cannot interact with other systems.
AI agents, on the other hand, are designed specifically for this type of work.
How Clawdi Uses the AI Agent Model

By combining conversational interaction with real-world actions, tools like Clawdi aim to bring the benefits of AI agents into everyday productivity.
